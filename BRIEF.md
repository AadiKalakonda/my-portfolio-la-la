# Portfolio Brief — Adarsh Kalakonda

## Who I am
Adarsh Kalakonda — MS Data Science candidate at SUNY Albany (graduating May 2026, 3.8 GPA). 2+ years as an AI & Data Analytics Engineer at Accenture, plus research on Graph Neural Networks at Anurag University. I build end-to-end: data pipelines, anomaly detection systems, LLM integrations, and the dashboards/visualizations that make the results legible to humans.

## What the site should communicate
- I'm technically deep (GNNs, PyTorch Geometric, LLM sequence analysis with Ollama, AWS pipelines) AND I ship things people actually use (Streamlit dashboards, Tableau reports, automated workflows).
- I care about the visualization and communication layer, not just the modeling layer. The site itself is evidence of this.
- I'm comfortable across the stack — data engineering, ML/DL, GenAI, MLOps, and enough frontend (Three.js, Flask, Django, Streamlit) to build my own interfaces.

## Audience
Primary: recruiters and hiring managers for data science, ML engineering, and applied AI roles in the US (post-May 2026).
Secondary: research collaborators, potential co-founders, other DS folks who might want to connect.

## Tone
Confident, technical, a little playful. Not corporate-sterile. Not "personal blog" either. Think editorial developer portfolio — serious about the work, with visual signature that shows taste.

## Visual direction
- Parallax + scroll-driven animation as the organizing metaphor
- Three.js hero: animated graph/network (direct nod to GNN work)
- Dark base (likely), with one or two accent colors used with restraint
- Type-forward — real typography, not default system fonts
- Metrics front and center — the numbers (91% accuracy, 1.4M+ records, 30-35% reduction, 50K+ node graph) are the proof points, not afterthoughts

## Don'ts
- No centered hero with two CTA buttons
- No generic gradient backgrounds or glassmorphism-for-its-own-sake
- No icon-grid "skills" section with colored rounded squares
- No "Hi, I'm X 👋" opener
- No generic SaaS-template vibe
- No light mode by default if dark feels right for the tone
